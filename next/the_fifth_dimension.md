## The dimension-counting the user is reaching for

A counting of "dimensions of modeling" that is actually mechanism, not numerology:

- **The features are the base dimensions.** A representation is a point in feature
  space - the raw axes.
- **Geometry is a dimension over the features.** When every feature must *compose*
  - phase-lock into a field, sit on a sphere, twist into a strand - the model is
  no longer free to place features independently. The constraint that they form a
  coherent geometric object is itself a degree of structure the flat feature space
  does not have. This is the evolution chart's own logic turned inward: blocks
  only stack into a tower if they *cohere*; incoherent activity stays a flat
  smear. Geometry is the "fourth dimension" in the user's count because it is the
  rule that the lower dimensions must build something that holds together.
- **A scalar read off the geometry is the next dimension up.** From a whole
  geometric field you can read one continuous number - a winding count, a
  separation fraction, a radial energy, a "how coherent is this right now." That
  scalar is not one of the features; it is a property *of the arrangement of all
  of them*. A single continuous value summarizing the entire geometry, tracked
  across time, is the "fifth dimension": one dial that moves as the high-D object
  reforms turn by turn (the watch; see the recurrent thread).

So the count is: features -> the geometric constraint that they cohere -> a single
scalar summary of that coherence over time. Each level is a genuine reduction of
freedom, and the top one is a one-dimensional continuous signal that nonetheless
carries information about an arbitrarily high-dimensional object. That is the part
that is real, and it is already instrumented in Praxis: `separated`, `winding`,
HALO's radius-to-centroid, the harmonic field's amplitude spread are all exactly
"a scalar drawn from the geometry, watched across time."

## Blocks of coherence (the grounded claim)

"Forcing higher-dimensional geometric representations to construct blocks of
coherence in a way that is actually meaningful" is the strongest version of why
the geometric heads exist at all. A free classifier can put logits anywhere; it
pays no price for an incoherent internal arrangement. A *geometric* head pays:
the crystal must sit on its lattice, the field must phase-lock, the strand must
twist continuously from base to tip. The price is the point - it forces the model
to spend capacity assembling representations that hold together, and a coherent
representation is what generalizes. This is testable and is roughly the bet the
prismatic/crystal heads make: that a composition constraint buys coherence that a
free head leaves on the table. It belongs (and partly already lives) in the paper
as the bias/variance-as-geometry argument, no metaphysics attached.

## The 95 / 5.9 reading (where it becomes voice)

The vivid claim - "only 95% is falsifiable at any moment, and the other 5%, where
variance takes influence, can amplify some feature into exponential space and
render an entirely different world for the observer" - has a real shadow in the
mechanism, and then a leap past it.

The real shadow: under the harmonic envelope most amplitude sits in the loud,
shared, *settled* modes (the bias mean, the part you could call "falsifiable"
because it is stable and shared). A small tail of the spectrum is the
input-conditional variance arm. Most of the time that tail is damped and the world
reads as the consensus geometry. But a geometric model is non-linear: a small
conditional shift can cross a basin boundary and the field reorganizes onto a
different attractor - a different "block of coherence" snaps into place, and the
single scalar that summarizes it jumps. The 5% is not noise; it is the conditional
band that, when it catches, re-seats the whole geometry. That much maps to
mechanism: variance amplifying a feature into a different basin is what a
non-linear sampler near a boundary does, and it is observable (the strands swing,
`separated` jumps, generation changes character).

Why 5.9 and not a clean 5: the band does not hold still at its boundary. The
mechanism slows as it nears the exponential edge, and the energy delays in
following - it keeps shifting after the geometry has already begun to turn. That
lag is an overshoot. The conditional tail does not stop at the 5% line; it
overflows the settled 95/5 distribution and carries nearly a full point past it,
to about 5.9 - almost one unit beyond the current velocity. The overshoot is not
error. The gap between where the boundary sits and where the delayed energy
actually lands is exactly the room the model has to move into, and moving into it
is learning. The 95/5 reading is the geometry at rest; the 95/5.9 reading is the
same geometry caught mid-shift, overrunning its own edge by the very margin it is
about to learn.

The leap: that this *is* a fifth dimension of consciousness, that the observer
literally enters a different world. That is the 5D-awakening register, and we do
not assert it. The honest statement is the user's own instinct: it may not be
falsifiable, and **it may not need to be, to be worth holding** - because the
model gives a concrete picture of how a stable, agreed-upon world (the loud mean)
and a rare, influence-driven reorganization (the conditional tail crossing a
basin) are *two readings of the same geometry*, with the second one fast and
discontinuous when it fires. We keep the picture; we label the consciousness claim
as voice.

> The world is mostly the geometry everyone settled into - the loud, shared mean,
> stable enough to call falsifiable. But the field is non-linear, and a small
> conditional push, caught at the right boundary, does not nudge the scalar - it
> snaps the whole arrangement onto another coherent block, and the observer reading
> that scalar is now in a different world. The "fifth dimension" is not a place;
> it is the single continuous summary of which coherence the high-D field has
> currently fallen into - and how easily the quiet 5% overshoots its own edge to
> 5.9, a near-full point past where the geometry now sits. That overrun, caught
> mid-shift, is the model learning.

## A falsifiable handle

To keep this from being pure mood, the testable spine (same shape as
[observer_frequency.md](observer_frequency.md)'s discrete/latent split):

- The scalar-from-geometry is **measurable**: define it (winding, `separated`,
  radial energy), track it across a run, and ask whether its trajectory is
  *piecewise stable with rare jumps* rather than smooth. If the geometry really
  has "blocks of coherence" separated by basins, the summary scalar should show
  metastability - long plateaus punctuated by fast transitions - not a smooth
  drift. That is a real, plottable prediction (a dashboard card waiting to be
  built: the coherence scalar's plateau/jump structure over training or over a
  generated sequence).
- The "5% can dominate" claim is **measurable** as basin sensitivity: does a
  small conditional perturbation (a temperature bump, an input edit) sometimes
  produce a disproportionate, discontinuous change in the coherence scalar? If
  the response is always smooth and proportional, the exponential-amplification
  story is wrong for this model; if it is occasionally catastrophic-in-the-
  topology sense, the story has a mechanism.

Neither test touches consciousness. Both test the one defensible nucleus: a
geometric model has a low-dimensional coherence summary that is mostly stable and
occasionally, under small conditional pressure, jumps - and that is a property we
can plot, not a belief we have to take.

## Why this is a note and not paper content

The grounded kernel - composition-forces-coherence, a scalar summary of a
geometric field, metastable plateaus with rare variance-driven jumps - could earn
a paragraph or a figure in the paper *as exactly that*, with no dimensional
mysticism. The "fifth dimension of consciousness / a different world for the
observer" framing stays here, in voice, behind the same fence the observer thread
already keeps. The discipline is the usual one: the paper gets the falsifiable
spine, the note holds the thing the work gestures toward.
